Weighted Upside-down Pull Up

The upside-down pullup is an intermediate exercise performed in calisthenics and gymnastics. It is typically performed on a pair of gymnastic rings, though it can also be performed by improvising with standard gym equipment. This exercise works strengthens the lats primarily, along with the biceps, upper back, and core. This variation is performed with a weighted vest for added tension.

Insructions

  1. Begin hanging from the gymnastic rings or gym equipment
  2. Keeping a tight core, raise your legs until you are in an inverted position with a neutral or supinated grip
  3. Using your legs to drive the motion, climb up the wall and walk your chest towards the wall with your hands
  4. "Walk" up the wall until you are nearly completely inverted, using your core to maintain a hollow body position with straight arms
  5. Keeping a tight core, lower your body by bending your elbows until they are parallel with the ground
  6. Once parallel, push through your hands to extend your elbows until your arms are straight again
  7. Repeat as many times as you are safely able and "walk" back down the wall to dismount
