The Deadlift

The deadlift is an extremely popular (though potentially dangerous) lower body exercise. To this day, bodybuilders argue about if it is the best vs the barbell squat. The deadlift, when done correctly, is highly beneficial. It targets the glutes, the hamstrings, the upper and lower back muscles, and the core. Additionally, it may be a better exercise for people with knee pain, whereas the squat may irritate said knees. The downside of the deadlift is that the technique to properly perform it is not as beginner-friendly as the squat. Amoung many other coordination factors, it requires the individual to be able to properly hinge at the hips, which itself must be practiced if living a sedentary lifestyle. It is recommended that individuals looking to deadlift consult a certified fitness trainer as well as their physician.

Insructions

  1. Begin by standing behind the barbell with your feet shoulder-width apart.
  2. Your shins should almost touch the bar (approximately the width of one finger away in distance)
  3. Hinge at the hips while maintaining a straight back. You should not be bending your knees just yet. For reference, start with your hands on your quads as you hinge.
  4. Once your hands reach your knee caps or you can't hinge any lower, extend your arms down towards the barbell and slowly bend at the knees until you can wrap your hands around the barbell with the grip of your choice.
  5. Contract your lats
  6. While maintaining a flat back, press through the ground with your legs to lift the bar. You should be merely holding the bar with your hands, not trying to lift with them.
  7. Once the bar passes your knees, drive with your hips with a flat back and contracted lats to continue lifting the bar until your legs are straight and your shoulders are back. Your knees do not need to be locked out.
  8. Return to the starting position by hinging the hips with a straight back, bending at the knees once be bar passes them, and then bending the knees until the bar touches the floor.
  9. repeat as many times as desired
