Windshield Wipers (Hanging)

The windshield wiper is a hanging bar abdominal exercise focused on strengthening the oblique and rectus abdominus muscles through rotation. Those muscles in turn need support from the glutes and hip flexor muscles. The hanging variant of this exercise is very challenging, requiring grip strength to hang onto the pull up bar and more core strength than the grounded variant due to having to stabilize the torso in a parallel position relative to the ground.
Beginners should start with the lying variant and develop their grip strength for hanging before attempting the hanging variant. A common mistake people make is to abuse the momentum gained by the legs while rotating side to side, taking the work away from the abs and creating what is almost a light bouncing motion. Care should be made to move slowly and deliberately while squeezing the abs in order to gain the maximum benefit from the work done.

Insructions

  1. Begin by suspending yourself from the pull up bar.
  2. Squeeze your abs together and lift your legs to pull your body into an L-shape
  3. Pull or swing your body so that your torso is roughly parallel to the ground and hold the position
  4. Keep your abs tight and slowly rotate your lower torso from side to side for a safe, but challenging number of repetitions
  5. Bring your legs back in line with your torso, lower them until you are in a regular hanging position, and dismount from the bar
