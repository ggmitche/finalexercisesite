Weighted Chin Up View

The weighted chin up is a challenging, but accessible exercise that works the biceps, forearms, and lats while increasing grip strength. This exercise is used to increase your ability to pull your body upwards under increased load after reaching diminishing returns with standard chin ups. As an added benefit it can increase shoulder mobility due to the stretch brought on by the added load. As a bonus, the exercise can be done with a weighted vest, a belt with weight fastened to it, or a single dumbbell.

Insructions

  1. Begin by suspending yourself from a pull up bar or gymnastic rings with your palms facing towards you
  2. Lean slightly backward or push your chest outward towards the bar
  3. Maintain that angle while pulling your elbows down towards your ribs in order to pull yourself upwards
  4. Pull yourself upward until either your chest touches the bar or you reach your maximum range of motion
  5. Lower yourself down into the hanging position and repeat
  6. Take care to avoid swinging your legs in order to benefit more from the exercise and avoid swinging the plate (if using a weighted belt)
