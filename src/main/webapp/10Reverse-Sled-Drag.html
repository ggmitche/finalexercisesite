Reverse Sled Walk

The reverse sled walk is a very effective leg exercise that is rapidly becoming more popular with young athletes and the elderly alike. The reverse sled drag is a powerful tool used to develop the calves, glutes, hamstrings, quadriceps, and the lower back in one neat package. It is very versatile, lending itself to either fat burning ( less weight with faster movement) or strength development (more weight with more deliberate movement). Even if you don't go to a gym, you can simulate the exercise by making a sled or fastening chains to a weighted object. If your hips are too sensitive for any reason, you can substitute this exercise with the reverse sled drag where the rope or chain is fastened to a hand-held handle.

Insructions

  1. Begin by safely fastening the rope or chains to your waist
  2. While leaning slightly backwards with your torso, begin stepping backwards until the rope or chains are taut
  3. Continue moving backwards at a pace that is reasonable for you at the weight you have chosen
  4. Keep your knees and toes pointed towards the sled so that your joints track naturally throughout the exercise
