Front Lever Raise

The front lever raise is a hard to learn, harder to master exercise that is a pillar of both calisthenics and gymnastics. The exercise works an astounding amount of muscles. It works approximately 5 back muscles in the back alone. Other muscle groups involved include the chest, shoulders, arms, core, hips, and legs. The front lever raise demands a lot of patience to work up to and itself is yet another step to progress to the front lever hold. It demands even more patience from taller and larger individuals, as every inch adds extra footpounds of downward force to be counterracted.

Insructions

  1. Begin by suspending yourself from the pull-up bar in a dead hang position with the legs straight
  2. Keeping your arms straight, raise your legs while leaning your upper body backward
  3. Retract your shoulder blades and continue to raise your legs until your body is parallel to the ground with your toes are pointed outward
  4. Keeping your legs straight, reverse the movement to lower your body back into the hanging position
