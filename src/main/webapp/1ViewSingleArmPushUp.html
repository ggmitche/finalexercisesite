Single Arm Push-Up

The single arm push up is a very demanding calisthenics exercise that works the chest, shoulder, and tricep muscles. The core muscles, particularly the obliques and hip flexors are thrown in for good measure. The lack of support forces the core to work harder in order to stabilize the body throughout the movement. When done properly, the single arm push up creates full body tension and helps you develop more control on specific parts of the body while identifying and removing muscle imbalances in the muscles worked. Patience and progression are necessary in order to avoid improper form and injuries.

Instructions

  1. Begin by entering the standard push up position with a straight back and your hips over your knees
  2. Place your hands shoulder-width apart. The legs should be the same or slightly wider
  3. Take your other hand off the ground and place that arm next to your side or behind your back
  4. Slowly lower your chest towards the ground by bending your elbow
  5. Lower your body until your elbow is almost even with your back and pause briefly
  6. Raise your upper body by pushing into the floor and straightening your elbow. Be sure to keep your body aligned properly until you reach the top
  7. Repeat as many times as you are safely able and change arms
