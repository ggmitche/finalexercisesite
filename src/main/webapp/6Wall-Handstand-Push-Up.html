Wall Handstand Push Up (Facing Wall)

The wall handstand push up is a fun, challenging exercise, and an important stepping stone to an unsupported handstand push up. This exercise works the shoulders, chest, arms, and back muscles with a little bit of core thrown in. The wall handstand push up demands great strength as well as the ability to control your body from an inverted position. The variation facing the wall is easier to get into than facing away from the wall, but may be harder to execute for some

Insructions

  1. Begin by facing away from the wall and dropping down on all fours
  2. Place your hands shoulder-width apart and kick one or both legs back to touch the wall
  3. Using your legs to drive the motion, climb up the wall and walk your chest towards the wall with your hands
  4. "Walk" up the wall until you are nearly completely inverted, using your core to maintain a hollow body position with straight arms
  5. Keeping a tight core, lower your body by bending your elbows until they are parallel with the ground
  6. Once parallel, push through your hands to extend your elbows until your arms are straight again
  7. Repeat as many times as you are safely able and "walk" back down the wall to dismount
